Republic Act No. 4828
June 18, 1966
AN ACT ESTABLISHING A FRESHWATER FISH FARM AND HATCHERY IN BARRIO LOOC, MUNICIPALITY OF CARDONA, PROVINCE OF RIZAL, AND AUTHORIZING THE APPROPRIATION OF FUNDS THEREFOR, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES.


Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. There shall be established and maintained, a freshwater fish farm and hatchery under the supervision of the Philippine Fisheries Commission at a suitable site along the lake foreshore of Barrio Looc, Municipality of Cardona, Province of Rizal, to be known as the Rizal FreshWater Fish Farm and Hatchery, which shall primarily perform the following functions:

  1. (a) To conduct experiments in various aspects of freshwater fish culture;

    (b) To cultivate, propagate and disseminate various economic and valuable species of freshwater fish for culture and stocking purposes; and

    (c) To serve as a demonstration station in fish farming; import modern freshwater fish culture and propagation techniques; extend technical assistance and extension services; conduct development surveys, lectures, meetings and seminars; engage in cooperating activities with other government entities; and do other related work.

SEC. 2. The Commissioner of Fisheries shall promulgate such rules and regulations not inconsistent with law as may be necessary to carry into effect the purpose of this Act.

SEC. 3. The sum of two hundred fifty thousand pesos is hereby authorized to be appropriated, out of any funds in the National Treasury not otherwise appropriated, for the establishment, operation and maintenance of the freshwater fish farm and hatchery during the fiscal year nineteen hundred sixty-seven. Such sums as may be necessary for the operation and maintenance of the project for the succeeding years shall be included in the annual General Appropriations Act.

SEC. 4. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Approved, June 18, 1966.
