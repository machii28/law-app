Republic Act No. 7225
March 12, 1992
AN ACT EXTENDING THE FRANCHISE GRANTED TO THE IGLESIA NI CRISTO TO CONSTRUCT, INSTALL, ESTABLISH AND OPERATE RADIO STATIONS FOR PRIVATE TELECOMMUNICATIONS UNDER REPUBLIC ACT NUMBERED FORTY-THREE HUNDRED AND THIRTY-NINE TO ANOTHER TWENTY-FIVE YEARS
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. The life of the franchise of the Iglesia ni Cristo to construct, install, establish and operate radio stations for private telecommunications under Republic Act Numbered Forty-three hundred and thirty-nine is hereby extended to another twenty-five (25) years from February 10, 1994.

SEC. 2. All other provisions, terms and conditions contained in Republic Act Numbered Forty-three hundred and thirty-nine which are otherwise not affected hereby are made integral parts of this Act.

SEC. 3. The franchise hereby granted shall be subject to amendment, alteration or repeal by the Congress of the Philippines when the common good so requires.

SEC. 4. In addition to the terms and conditions herein provided, the Iglesia ni Cristo, shall submit an annual report to the Congress of the Philippines on its compliance with the terms and conditions of the franchise and on its operations within sixty (60) days from the end of every year.

SEC. 5. The grantee shall comply with and be subject to the provisions of a general telecommunications policy law that may hereafter be enacted.

SEC. 6. This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days from the date of its publication in at least two (2) newspapers of general circulation in the Philippines.


Approved,

(Sgd.) NEPTALI A. GONZALES (Sgd.) RAMON V. MITRA
President of the Senate Speaker of the House of Representatives

This Act which originated in the House of Representatives was finally passed by the House of Representatives and the Senate on January 15, 1992 and December 17, 1991, respectively.
