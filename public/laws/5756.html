Republic Act No. 5756
June 21, 1969
AN ACT TO EXTEND THE PROVISIONS OF REPUBLIC ACT NUMBERED FOUR THOUSAND EIGHT HUNDRED AND NINETY-EIGHT TO BARRIO TREASURERS AND BARRIO SECRETARIES, BY AMENDING SECTIONS ONE, TWO, THREE, FIVE, SIX AND SEVEN AND THE TITLE THEREOF.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Section one of Republic Act Numbered Four thousand eight hundred ninety-eight is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SECTION 1. Every barrio captain and councilman duly elected, and every barrio treasurer and barrio secretary duly appointed and qualified, under existing laws, but only while assuming their respective offices, are automatically covered by life, disability and accident insurance benefits herein provided which the Government Service Insurance System shall extend, as it is hereby directed to extend."
SEC. 2. Section two of the said Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SEC. 2. The life of a barrio captain is insured for five thousand pesos while that of a councilman, barrio treasurer or barrio secretary is insured for three thousand pesos each."
SEC. 3. Section three of the said Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SEC. 3. When a barrio captain, councilman, barrio treasurer or barrio secretary becomes fully and permanently incapacitated or disabled arising out of and in the course of the performance of his duties, the barrio captain shall be paid disability insurance proceeds of three thousand pesos, and the councilman, barrio treasurer or barrio secretary, in the amount of two thousand pesos each. Anyone availing the benefits under this Section cannot benefit from Sections four and five hereof."
SEC. 4. Section five of the said Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SEC. 5. Should a councilman, barrio treasurer or barrio secretary suffer injuries as a result of an accident, he shall be paid accident insurance proceeds as follows:
  1. For an injury to the brain resulting in the loss of sanity, three thousand pesos;
  2. For permanent loss of sight in both eyes, three thousand pesos;
  3. For permanent loss of sense of hearing in both ears, two thousand pesos;
  4. For permanent loss of sight in one eye, one thousand five hundred pesos;
  5. For permanent loss of sense of hearing in one ear, (f) For the loss or destruction of two or more limbs, three thousand pesos;
  6. For the loss of one limb, one thousand five hundred pesos;
  7. For the loss or destruction of both his hands at or above the wrist, two thousand pesos;
  8. For the loss or destruction of both his feet at or above the ankle, two thousand pesos;
  9. For the loss or destruction of one hand at or above the wrist, one thousand pesos;
  10. For the loss or destruction of one foot at or above the ankle, one thousand pesos;
  11. For the loss or destruction of every thumb or great toe, one hundred pesos;
  12. For the loss or destruction of every finger or toe, eighty pesos;
  13. For any other injury requiring hospitalization of at least forty days, seven hundred pesos, and in cases where the hospitalization is less than forty days, at such amount not exceeding six hundred fifty pesos as determined by the Government Service Insurance System and prescribed in its Rules and Regulations issued pursuant to the provisions of this Act; and
  14. For any other injury requiring medical attention for at least fifteen days, one hundred pesos, and in cases where the medical attention extended is less than fifteen days, at such amount not exceeding ninety pesos as determined by the Government Services Insurance System and prescribed in its Rules and Regulations, issued pursuant to the provisions of this Act."
SEC. 5. Section six of the same Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SEC. 6. For all the insurance benefits provided under this Act, the national government of the Republic of the Philippines shall pay an annual premium to the Government Service Insurance System the sum of fifteen pesos for every barrio captain and the sum of ten pesos for every councilman, barrio treasurer or barrio secretary: Provided, That any failure or delay in the payment of such premiums shall not in any way prejudice the efficacy and enforceability of the insurance benefits herein set forth."
SEC. 6. Section seven of the same Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"SEC. 7. To carry out the purposes of this Act, there is hereby authorized to be appropriated out of any funds in the national treasury not otherwise appropriated, the amount of two million eight hundred fifty thousand pesos to pay the annual premiums starting upon the approval of this Act, Thereafter, there shall be authorized to be appropriated annually and included in the General Appropriations Act for every subsequent year, out of any funds in the National Treasury not otherwise appropriated, the amount of two million eight hundred fifty thousand pesos or such amount as may be necessary to pay the annual premiums payable by the national government by virtue of this Act as certified to by the Presidential Assistant on Community Development: Provided, That any of such amount not used to pay said premiums shall automatically revert to the general fund."
SEC. 7. The title of the same Act is hereby amended to read as follows:
"An Act Providing Instance Coverage To Barrio Captains, Councilmen, Barrio Treasurers and Barrio Secretaries, and Authorizing the appropriation of funds for the purposes."
SEC. 8. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.

Approved, June 21, 1969.
